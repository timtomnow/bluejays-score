When the Blue Jays are playing, the top card turns into a live scoreboard that updates on its own. This guide explains what each part of it means.

## Steps

1. Open the app during a game. The top card shows a red **Live** badge.
2. Read the **score** for each team in the score row, with each team's record below it.
3. Check the **Count**, **Bases**, and **Outs** in the situation row — balls and strikes as dots, the runners on the base diamond, and the number of outs.
4. Below that, read the **batter vs pitcher** matchup, with each player's name, throwing/batting hand, and role (**Batting** or **Pitching**).
5. Read the **last play** line under the matchup for the most recent result.
6. Scroll the card for the **Line Score** (runs, hits, and errors by inning), the venue, and TV broadcasts.

## What you'll see

A live scoreboard that refreshes about every 30 seconds, so the score, count, bases, and matchup stay current without a manual refresh. When the game ends, the badge changes to **Final** and the **Win**, **Loss**, and **Save** decisions appear.
